Dankalia

The Southern Red Sea Region (Italian: Regione del Mar Rosso Meridionale) is a region of Eritrea. It lies along the southern half of the Red Sea, and contains the coastal city of Assab. It borders the Northern Red Sea Region, and has an area of around 27,600 km2 (10,700 sq mi).

As of 2005, the region had a population of 83,500 compared to a population of 73,700 in 2001. The net growth rate was 11.74 per cent. The total area of the province was 27600.00 km2 and the density was 3.03 persons per km2

Nefasit-Dongolo

Nefasit (Arabic: نفاسيت‎, Tigrinya: ነፋሲት Tigrinya pronunciation: [nɐfasit]), also known as Tappa Nefasit, is a small town in the Northern Red Sea Region of Eritrea. Monastery of Debre Bizen is located at the mountains above. According to multiple tour groups the stretch between the capital Asmara and Nefasit is the most beautiful in Eritrea. There's a colonial era Italian railway that occasionally runs from Asmara.

  Population

  Geograph
Coastline: 2,234 km total; mainland on Red Sea 1,151 km, islands in Red Sea 1,083 km. Border countries: Djibouti 125 km, Ethiopia 1,033 km, Sudan 682 km.The Dahlak Archipelago is situated off the sandy and arid coastline
 Climate
Eritrea can be divided into 3 major climate zones : the central highlands, the coastal region, and the western lowlands & have either a hot semi-arid climate or a hot desert climate, although temperatures are much moderated at the highest elevations.

  Language
There are nine languages in Eritrea. Tigrinya (50%) and Arabic are the working languages. The other languages are Tigre (40%), Afar (4%), Saho (3%), Bega (Beja), Bilen, Nara and Kunama. English and Italian are also widely understood.
  History
Following referendum in Eritrea in which the Eritrean people overwhelmingly voted for independence and gained recognition in 1993. The EPLF seized power, and banned any political activity.have been no elections since.
  UNESCO
On 8 July 2017, the entire capital city of Asmara, (Known by italians as Piccolo Rome ) was listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with the inscription taking place during the 41st World Heritage Committee Session.
  Transportation
The Eritrean Ropeway, completed in 1937, ran 71.8 km extended from Asmara - Massawa. This was the longest cableway ever. It covered a distance which was over twice the current one longest in the world which has less than 35 km in Lapland.
